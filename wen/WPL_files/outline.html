The sparc and initiation of the project
  • …came in the summer of 2003, when Professor Timothy Murray was introduced to Wen Pulin by the curator Yang Shin-yi in Beijing. Tim visited Wen’s Dongtai Institute where the archive – then in its analog form – was housed, and took quick stock of the materials. After his return from China, Tim started to discuss the possibility to digitize the materials – consisting of over 400 tapes in various formats on events related to Chinese modern art and culture. These tapes recorded

    - art happenings all over China;
    - exhibitions, those both officially sanctioned  _and underground;
    - the very first Chinese rock concerts;
  • - modern Tibetan cultural expressions;
    - interviews with artists;
    - explorations of the new urban landscapes of _major Chinese cities;
    - the discourse surrounding many of the _(sometimes highly controversial) exhibitions, _and their social context.

    All this over a period between 1985 to ca. 2005, a period of time many say are the most important 20 years ever in Chinese history.
