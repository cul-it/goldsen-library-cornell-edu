The technical specs
(if anyone cares to ask):
- ca. 405 tapes were digitized (Sony beta max tapes, ¾ inch tapes, various out-dated, other formats). - Oldest tapes from 1985. The colors had faded out, so we produced b/w digital copies. - Audio is usually very clear, except with some older tapes, where we only have mono, instead of stereo coverage. The Archive exists in two forms:
a) on 147 DVD (originally 274 DVD), with all files in the DVD video format, with a resolution of usually 720x576; and
b) on a 400GB hard drive (using up ca. 340GB) , with high quality MPEG-4 AVI/DivX  formatted files also with a resolution of 720 x 576,  which were converted with the Microsoft MPEG4 encoder V2, ensuring that the MPEG-4 files are fully compatible with Windows Media Player (the most widely used player for such file types) for normal users. - The equipment necessary to push these very large files out to patrons would be a windows streaming video fileserver, which we’ll set up in the near future.
